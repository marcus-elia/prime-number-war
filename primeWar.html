Welcome to The Prime Number War

Overview

This is a single player game where you compete against the computer. You are on the left side of the screen, and the computer is on the right. In the middle, there are 63 hexagons that have random integers on them. The object of the game is to have the highest score when there are no hexagons left.

Points

You score points by launching missiles and hitting hexagons. When a missile hits a hexagon, the hexagon and the missile are both destroyed. If the number on the hexagon was prime, you gain that number of points. If the number was composite, that number gets added to each of the hexagons bordering the hexagon you just hit. If a number becomes greater than 100, it wraps back around. So 95 + 16 makes 11.

If your missile hits the computer and it currently has more points than you, you steal one third of the difference between your point values. For example, if you have 100 points and the computer has 130 points, you will gain 10 and the computer will lose 10. The same applies if the computer's missile hits you. If two missiles collide, they will both be destroyed. You can use this to defend yourself.

Controls

You can move up and down with the W and S keys, respectively. At any time, you can launch a missile by clicking on the screen. The missile will be launched from your location toward the spot where you clicked. If you click while you already have a missile, the previous missile will vanish and a new one will be launched. You can steer your missiles using the A and D keys. Holding A will make it curve counterclockwise, and holding D will make it curve clockwise.
